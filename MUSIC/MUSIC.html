CARNATIC MUSIC:

Carnatic music is a traditional style of music that originated in southern India. It's a form of classical music that's rich in melody and rhythm. In Carnatic music, singers use their voice and musicians play instruments like the violin, flute, veena, and mridangam.The music is structured around ragas (melodic scales) and talas (rhythmic patterns). Musicians improvise within these structures, creating intricate melodies and rhythms. Carnatic music often involves intricate patterns, known as 'ragam,' which establish the melody, and 'talam,' which establishes the rhythm.

HINDUSTANI MUSIC:

Hindustani music is a traditional style of music from North India. It's a beautiful blend of melody, rhythm, and emotion. It involves singing (usually accompanied by instruments) and follows specific scales called ragas and rhythms called taal.In Hindustani music, there are different ragas, each with its unique mood and set of notes. These ragas are like musical recipes, guiding the musician on which notes to play and how to express different emotions.

FOLK MUSIC:

Folk music is like a musical storyteller from the past. It's the kind of music that regular people in a certain place or culture would create and sing. It's often passed down through generations, carrying stories, traditions, and emotions of a community.Folk music can be about everyday life, historical events, or cultural celebrations. It's usually played with traditional instruments and reflects the unique identity and experiences of a specific group of people.

POP MUSIC:

Pop music is a type of popular music that's catchy and easy to enjoy. It often features simple melodies, repetitive lyrics, and a rhythmic beat that makes you want to dance or sing along. Pop songs are created to be widely liked and get played on the radio a lot.They usually focus on everyday feelings and experiences, making them relatable to many people. Pop music often includes a mix of different musical styles and is known for its memorable tunes that can quickly get stuck in your head.
